**Document checks**:

### Treasure Distribution Best Practices

**Balance Checklist**:
- [ ] Total treasure across all sessions matches level guidelines (no hoarding treasure in early sessions)
- [ ] Magic item progression matches party level (no +3 sword at level 2)
- [ ] Rare items are unique and significant (each rare item has narrative weight, not just mechanical bonus)
- [ ] Treasure doesn't trivialize encounters (no Cloak of Invisibility given before it would break stealth challenges)
- [ ] Multiple characters get magic items (not just one PC with all loot)
- [ ] Loot distribution spreads across multiple sessions (not 500 gp dump in SESSION-2, then nothing)
- [ ] Boss encounters reward meaningfully (final boss loot is better than standard encounter)
- [ ] Consumable items (potions, scrolls) are available early for item flexibility
- [ ] Coin distribution sufficient for player agency (can PCs afford to craft/enchant items?)

**Red Flags**:
- âš ï¸ Party accumulates 2000+ gp and no major magic items by level 5 â†’ loot distribution too coin-heavy
- âš ï¸ Single rare item acquired, then no magic items for 5 sessions â†’ uneven distribution
- âš ï¸ Boss encounters award less loot than standard encounters â†’ incentivizes avoiding boss fights
- âš ï¸ Players can one-shot all enemies with found items â†’ treasures too powerful, rebalance
- âš ï¸ No consumable items (potions, scrolls, single-use spells) available â†’ limits player tactical options

## Save Point Strategy & Checkpoint Design (Computer Game Priority 1)

### Save System Architecture

**Activated when**: `[PLATFORM]` = "Computer Game" in constitution.md

**Auto-save Strategy**: Automatic saves at chapter boundaries and major story beats; players can manual-save at designated checkpoints.

#### Checkpoint Types

| Checkpoint Type | Purpose | Frequency | Effect | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Chapter Start** | Mandatory save point at chapter opening | 1 per chapter | Creates fresh save slot for chapter | CHAPTER-3 save before first node |
| **Story Beat** | Save before major story decision | 1-2 per chapter | Allows player to undo decision without replaying | Before romance choice, before faction commitment |
| **Safe Zone** | Save in "safe" location (town, hub, safe house) | 1-2 per chapter | Establishes checkpoint for exploration | Temple saves, home base saves |
| **Encounter Checkpoint** | Save before major encounter | 1-2 per chapter | Allows encounter retry without full chapter replay | Before boss battle, before final puzzle |
| **Quick Save** | Manual player-initiated save (unlimited, last checkpoint only) | Player choice | Overwrites previous quick save | Player presses save button anytime |

#### Soft Save vs. Hard Save

**Hard Save** (Named Checkpoints):
- Chapter Start: "CHAPTER-3-START" (auto-saved, named)
- Story Beats: "CHAPTER-3-ROMANCE-CHOICE" (auto-saved, player can load)
- Safe Zones: "CHAPTER-3-TEMPLE-HUB" (auto-saved, manual)
- Encounter: "CHAPTER-3-BOSS-BATTLE" (auto-saved, checkpoint before boss)

**Soft Save** (Quick Save):
- Manual player save (player presses button)
- Overwrites previous quick save only
- Creates fallback if player wants to undo last decision before proceeding

#### State Persistence Across Saves

**Persistent State** (survives save/load):
- Companion approval (locks in, doesn't reset)
- Faction reputation (locks in)
- Items collected (persist in inventory)
- Route commitment (playthrough_route immutable)
- Accessibility settings (player preferences)
- Chapter milestone (can't go backward)

**Reset on Load** (starts from save point):
- NPC positions (reset to save-point state)
- Enemy health (encounter resets)
- Active debuffs (poisoned, cursed effects reset)
- Puzzle state (puzzle resets if not yet solved at save point)
- Timer state (timed challenges restart)
