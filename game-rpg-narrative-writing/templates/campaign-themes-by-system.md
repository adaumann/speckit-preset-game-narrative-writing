# Campaign Themes by Rule Set

This guide explains how the same narrative themes work differently across rule systems, and shows how mechanics emphasize different aspects.

---

## Overview: What is a Campaign Theme?

A **campaign theme** is the narrative and mechanical focus:
- **Narrative focus:** What stories are told (investigations, monsters, politics)
- **Mechanical focus:** Which dice rolls matter most (combat, social, investigation)
- **Pacing emphasis:** Combat vs. roleplay vs. exploration percentages
- **NPC types:** Who the party encounters and how they interact

The **same theme** (e.g., "Political Intrigue") feels completely different in different systems because the underlying mechanics emphasize different actions.

## D&D 5e Campaign Themes

### System Characteristics
- **Core mechanic:** d20 skill checks + attack rolls + HP pools
- **Strength:** Combat encounters, monster variety, level progression
- **Weakness:** Social interactions can feel mechanical
- **Best themes:** Combat-focused, exploration-focused

### D&D 5e Theme Matrix

| Theme | Description | Core Mechanic | Session Structure | PC Agency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Dungeon Delving** | Explore underground complex, solve puzzles, defeat monsters | Room-by-room exploration + monster combat | 60% combat, 30% exploration, 10% social | High (players choose dungeon path) |
| **Monster Hunt** | Track and defeat dangerous creatures | Investigation + combat preparation + boss fight | 50% combat, 30% investigation, 20% social | Medium (following creature trail) |
| **Political Intrigue** | Navigate factional conflicts, broker deals | Persuasion checks, faction reputation tracking | 30% combat, 50% social, 20% exploration | High (many negotiation paths) |
| **Heist** | Plan and execute theft/infiltration | Stealth checks, social deception, combat as backup | 40% combat, 40% social/stealth, 20% prep | Very high (planning phases) |
| **Investigation** | Solve mystery, find culprits | Skill checks (Insight, Investigation, Perception) | 20% combat, 60% investigation, 20% social | High (multiple investigation paths) |
| **Curse Breaking** | Discover and lift magical curse | Arcane knowledge, spell use, ritual magic | 40% combat, 40% exploration, 20% social | Medium (following magical clues) |
| **Defense** | Defend settlement/stronghold from siege/invasion | Waves of combat encounters + morale | 70% combat, 20% community, 10% strategy | Medium (defend chosen position) |
| **Open-Ended** | Player-directed adventure, no set theme | All mechanics available equally | 25% each (balanced) | Very high (player-created goals) |

### D&D 5e Mechanics by Theme

**Dungeon Delving:**
- Emphasis: Attack rolls, saving throws, trap DC checks
- Skip: Extended roleplay with NPCs
- Hooks: VISITED (rooms cleared), INVENTORY (treasure), TIMER (dungeon collapses)

**Political Intrigue:**
- Emphasis: Persuasion, Deception, Insight checks; faction reputation
- Skip: Long combat encounters (1-2 enemies max)
- Hooks: TRUST (NPC approval), COUNTER (faction rep), FLAG (conspiracy discovered)

**Heist:**
- Emphasis: Stealth, Deception, perception
- Skip: Protracted dungeon crawls
- Hooks: INVENTORY (target items), TIMER (guard patrol), COUNTER (heist prep completion)

**Monster Hunt:**
- Emphasis: Perception, Survival, Arcana/Nature for creature knowledge
- Skip: Political negotiations
- Hooks: FLAG (monster located), COUNTER (monsters_slain), INVENTORY (trophy/proof)

## PBTA (Powered by the Apocalypse) Campaign Themes

### System Characteristics
- **Core mechanic:** Move-based (when trigger happens, move happens); 2d6 + bonus
- **Strength:** Relationship dynamics, conversation-focused, quick pacing
- **Weakness:** Limited combat support, no levels/progression
- **Best themes:** Relationship-driven, community-focused

### PBTA Theme Matrix

| Theme | Description | Core Mechanic | Session Structure | PC Agency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Apocalypse Survival** | Survive harsh post-apocalyptic world, manage resources | Resources management + relationship moves | 20% combat, 30% foraging, 50% community | Very high (all choices matter) |
| **Monster Hunting** | Hunt supernatural/magical creatures in modern world | Stalking moves, investigation moves, combat | 40% investigation, 40% combat, 20% social | High (tracking possibilities) |
| **Intrigue Network** | Navigate underground networks, manage relationships | Social moves, negotiation, alliance-building | 10% combat, 70% social, 20% exploration | Very high (relationship-focused) |
| **Personal Stories** | Deep character arcs, relationship drama | Relationship moves, personal moves | 5% combat, 85% roleplay/social, 10% other | Very high (emotion-driven) |
| **Survival Horror** | Survive dangerous place with group | Resource management + panic moves | 30% combat, 40% resource mgmt, 30% roleplay | Medium (harsh consequences) |
| **Heist/Crew** | Build crew, plan and execute scores | Preparation moves, relationship building | 15% combat, 50% planning, 35% social | Very high (crew dynamics) |
| **Open Community** | Build community, manage collective safety | Group moves, resource tracking, long-term arcs | 5% combat, 70% community, 25% individual | High (community-driven) |

### PBTA Mechanics by Theme

**Apocalypse Survival:**
- Emphasis: Resource moves, community health, faction alliances
- Skip: Hero combat heroics (emphasize desperate survival)
- Hooks: COUNTER (resources), FLAG (community status), TRUST (ally approval)

**Monster Hunting:**
- Emphasis: "Tracking moves", investigation, creature lore
- Skip: Individual monsters shouldn't take 30 min of combat
- Hooks: FLAG (monster found), COUNTER (hunts completed), INVENTORY (trophies)

**Intrigue Network:**
- Emphasis: Conversation, alliances, betrayals
- Skip: Combat (rare, meaningful only)
- Hooks: TRUST (relationship status), FLAG (alliance formed), COUNTER (faction influence)

**Personal Stories:**
- Emphasis: Relationship moves, character moments, personal goals
- Skip: Large-scale conflicts (focus on personal impact)
- Hooks: TRUST (all NPC relationships), FLAG (personal goals), COUNTER (emotional progress)

## FATE Campaign Themes

### System Characteristics
- **Core mechanic:** Aspects (narrative conditions) + 4dF (Fudge dice) + skills
- **Strength:** Narrative flexibility, aspect-driven play, cinematic action
- **Weakness:** No HP tracking, combat abstract and deadly
- **Best themes:** Cinematic adventure, dramatic arcs

### FATE Theme Matrix

| Theme | Description | Core Mechanic | Session Structure | PC Agency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pulp Adventure** | Classic adventure across exotic locations, solve mysteries | Create/invoke Aspects, overcome obstacles | 30% combat, 40% challenges, 30% exploration | High (Aspect creation opens paths) |
| **Detective Mystery** | Investigate crimes, uncover conspiracies, confront villains | Clue-gathering challenges, social confrontations | 10% combat, 70% investigation, 20% social | Very high (investigation directions) |
| **Political Thriller** | Navigate power struggles, broker deals, prevent catastrophes | Social Aspects, relationship changes | 15% combat, 70% social, 15% intrigue | Very high (Aspect-driven politics) |
| **Supernatural Horror** | Investigate supernatural phenomena, survive occult threats | Investigation moves, horror Aspects, rare combat | 20% combat, 50% investigation, 30% horror | Medium (threats drive action) |
| **Heist/Caper** | Plan and execute complex schemes, outmaneuver rivals | Preparation + execution with Aspect complication | 20% combat, 60% planning/execution, 20% social | Very high (plan flexibility) |
| **Epic Warfare** | Fight across multiple locations, command troops | Large-scale conflict Aspects, tactical combat | 50% combat, 30% strategy, 20% between-battles | Medium (commander role) |
| **Romance Drama** | Navigate relationships, pursue love interests | Social Aspects, relationship changes | 5% combat, 80% roleplay, 15% outside conflict | Very high (relationship-driven) |

### FATE Mechanics by Theme

**Pulp Adventure:**
- Emphasis: Aspect creation (obstacles that open new paths), overcome challenges
- Skip: Extended HP tracking (combat quick and decisive)
- Hooks: FLAG (location discovered), COUNTER (mysteries solved), INVENTORY (Aspect objects)

**Detective Mystery:**
- Emphasis: Create Advantages (discovering Aspects about suspects), investigation
- Skip: Large combat encounters (mystery is the focus)
- Hooks: FLAG (truth discovered), COUNTER (clues gathered), INVENTORY (evidence)

**Political Thriller:**
- Emphasis: Invoke Aspects (using political situations), social interactions
- Skip: Combat (rare, narrative only)
- Hooks: TRUST (political alliance strength), FLAG (faction positions), COUNTER (influence)

**Heist/Caper:**
- Emphasis: Preparation (creating Aspects that help), execution (invoking them)
- Skip: Random encounters
- Hooks: FLAG (plan executed), COUNTER (preparation completion), INVENTORY (target items)

## Blades in the Dark Campaign Themes

### System Characteristics
- **Core mechanic:** Resistance rolls, Devil's Bargains, position/effect
- **Strength:** Criminal enterprise, faction play, resistance mechanics
- **Weakness:** Limited non-crime focus, 3-6 level cap
- **Best themes:** Criminal crew, city intrigue

### Blades in the Dark Theme Matrix

| Theme | Description | Core Mechanic | Session Structure | PC Agency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Criminal Enterprise** | Run criminal crew, plan scores, manage faction wars | Score planning, resistance rolls, faction moves | 20% combat, 40% planning, 40% roleplay | Very high (crew decisions) |
| **Ghost Field** | Deal with supernatural threats in city | Supernatural complications, resistance rolls | 30% combat, 30% investigation, 40% supernatural | High (ghost complications arise) |
| **Faction War** | Navigate complex faction politics, broker betrayals | Faction moves, political complications | 15% combat, 60% politics, 25% social | Very high (faction-driven) |
| **Heist Focus** | Execute elaborate heists against powerful targets | Score design, resistance rolls, consequences | 30% combat, 50% planning, 20% social | Very high (plan flexibility) |
| **Street Life** | Survive street politics, build reputation, avoid law | Street encounter moves, faction standing | 25% combat, 50% street encounter, 25% planning | High (reputation matters) |
| **Personal Vengeance** | Hunt targets, exact revenge, manage consequences | Personal Scores, resistance mechanics | 40% combat, 35% planning, 25% roleplay | Medium (targets drive action) |
| **City Takeover** | Build criminal empire, conquer turf, defeat rivals | Long-term campaign, faction wars | 35% combat, 40% politics, 25% planning | Medium (empire building) |

### Blades in the Dark Mechanics by Theme

**Criminal Enterprise:**
- Emphasis: Score planning (position/effect), faction clocks, crew upgrades
- Skip: Random encounter tables (use scores/faction moves)
- Hooks: FLAG (score succeeded/failed), COUNTER (faction reputation), INVENTORY (heat level)

**Ghost Field:**
- Emphasis: Supernatural complications, Leech abilities, spirit interactions
- Skip: Extended dungeon exploration
- Hooks: FLAG (ghost revealed), COUNTER (supernatural knowledge), INVENTORY (ritual items)

**Faction War:**
- Emphasis: Faction clocks, diplomatic moves, betrayal mechanics
- Skip: Random combat
- Hooks: COUNTER (faction reputation all 4), FLAG (faction status changes), TRUST (crew loyalty)

**Heist Focus:**
- Emphasis: Score design, position/effect, planning phases
- Skip: Extended downtime
- Hooks: FLAG (target acquired/escaped), COUNTER (heist prep completion), INVENTORY (target items)

## How to Adapt a Theme Between Systems

### Example: Political Intrigue in D&D 5e vs. FATE

#### D&D 5e Political Intrigue
```
System Focus: Combat + Social Skills
- Faction reputation tracked as numbers (-100 to +100)
- Dialogue choice determines Persuasion DC
- Combat can occur if negotiations fail
- Healing available, multiple sessions to recover
- Ending determined by faction reputation > 50 threshold
```

#### FATE Political Intrigue
```
System Focus: Narrative Aspects + Social Challenges
- Faction positions tracked as Aspects ("House A Ascendant", "Merchant Cabal Weakened")
- Dialogue creates Advantages (new Aspects) or causes complications
- Combat rare, consequence is elimination not HP loss
- No healing — defeated character is out (create new Aspect: "hiding from enemies")
- Ending determined by Aspects in play (invoking final Aspect)
```

#### PBTA Political Intrigue
```
System Focus: Relationship Moves + Community Health
- Factions tracked as "allegiances" and "debts"
- Dialogue triggers "make a connection" or "convince someone" moves (2d6)
- Combat very rare, handled by simple moves
- Community health clock tracks overall situation
- Ending determined by community health + relationship scores
```

## Customization Template: Adapting Themes

When moving a theme to a new system:

### Step 1: Identify Core Narrative
```
Theme: Political Intrigue
Core story: Navigate factional conflicts to achieve goal
Central question: Which faction will the party ally with?
Key decision: Choose who to trust
```

### Step 2: Map to System Mechanics
```
D&D 5e:        → faction_rep counters + Persuasion checks
FATE:          → faction Aspects + Create Advantage moves
PBTA:          → relationship debts + alliance moves
Blades:        → faction clocks + political complication moves
```

### Step 3: Adjust Session Structure
```
D&D 5e:        60% negotiation, 20% combat, 20% investigation
FATE:          70% challenge scenes, 20% social, 10% combat
PBTA:          80% relationship play, 15% movement/consequence
Blades:        50% score planning, 30% faction moves, 20% complications
```

### Step 4: Update NPC Behaviors
```
D&D 5e:        NPCs have explicit approval scores, react to reputation
FATE:          NPCs create Aspects that reflect relationships
PBTA:          NPCs trigger moves through their requests/demands
Blades:        NPCs have faction affiliations, drive scores
```

## Theme + System Synergy Examples

### Strong Synergy (Theme + System naturally reinforce each other)
- **Dungeon Delving + D&D 5e** ✅ (system designed for this)
- **Personal Stories + PBTA** ✅ (moves built for relationships)
- **Detective Mystery + FATE** ✅ (Aspects perfect for clues)
- **Criminal Enterprise + Blades** ✅ (core system)

### Good Synergy (Theme + System work but need some adjustment)
- **Investigation + D&D 5e** ✅ (need emphasis on skill checks over combat)
- **Heist + PBTA** ✅ (planning moves available)
- **Political Intrigue + FATE** ✅ (Aspects work for factions)
- **Monster Hunt + Blades** ✅ (as supernatural scores)

### Requiring Work (Theme + System need substantial modification)
- **Defense + PBTA** ⚠️ (no army mechanics, focus on community)
- **Dungeon Delving + FATE** ⚠️ (map dungeon as narrative Aspects)
- **Curse Breaking + PBTA** ⚠️ (no magic system, use supernatural moves)
- **Community Building + D&D 5e** ⚠️ (add downtime rules)

## Creating New Themes

Users can create custom themes by:

1. **Identify narrative focus** — What stories are told?
2. **Choose dominant mechanic** — Which system rolls happen most?
3. **Set session percentages** — Combat % / social % / exploration % / custom %
4. **Define NPC types** — Who do players meet?
5. **Specify endings** — What determines success/failure?

**Example: Monster Investigation Theme**

```
Narrative Focus: Hunt creatures AND uncover why they exist
Dominant Mechanic: Perception + Investigation + Combat
Session Mix: 30% combat, 40% investigation, 20% travel, 10% social
NPC Types: Beast hunters, naturalists, curse-breakers
Endings: Creature dead + curse lifted, creature controlled, curse spreads
```
