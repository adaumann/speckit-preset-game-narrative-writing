# The Shrine — Game Bible

A minimal narrative sample demonstrating dialogue-driven design with optional quest mechanics.

---

## Style Mode

**humanized-ai**

Focus on distinct priestess voice, meaningful dialogue branches, and emotional resonance over mechanical complexity.

---

## Core Mission

Create a 2-3 minute narrative experience where the player's dialogue choices and attributes (intelligence, wisdom, power, gold) gate different story paths. Demonstrate how narrative design validation works before exporting to SugarCube for testing.

## Character Profiles

### Player Character: "The Wanderer"

Starting attributes: INT 5, WIS 6, PWR 4, GLD 10

No fixed personality — player choices define arc.

### Priestess (NPC)

- Respectful, mysterious, knowing
- Values wisdom and insight
- Wary of arrogance (power-based demands)
- Recognizes Old Book (lore object)
- Grants blessing based on demonstrated wisdom or intelligence

**Trust Variable**: None (single-encounter, no relationship system)

---

## Quest: Seeking the Blessing

**Stages**: 3

**Stage 1**: Meet priestess, choose dialogue path
- Ask about shrine history (INT ≥ 6 reward)
- Donate to shrine (GOLD ≥ 5 reward)
- Search shrine (find Old Book)

**Stage 2**: Priestess responds based on Stage 1 choice

**Stage 3**: Conclusion — receive blessing or depart

**Completion Variables**:
- `$quest_shrine_blessing_stage` = 1|2|3
- `$quest_shrine_blessing_complete` = true/false

See `world/shrine/quest-shrine-blessing/overview.md` for full quest outline.

## Technical Notes

**Variables used**:
- `$character` — Object with intelligence, wisdom, power, gold
- `$inventory` — Array of {item, name, qty} objects
- `$visited_shrine` — Boolean
- `$spoke_priestess` — Boolean
- `$found_old_book` — Boolean
- `$quest_shrine_blessing_stage` — Integer (1, 2, or 3)
- `$quest_shrine_blessing_complete` — Boolean

**Hooks used**:
- `[MECHANIC:ATTRIBUTE]` — Check player stat gates in dialogue
- `[MECHANIC:INVENTORY]` — Pickup and check items
- `[MECHANIC:FLAG]` — Track visited locations and NPC interactions

**No hooks used**: No counters, no timers, no combat, no reputation tracking
